My Love Is Cool

The debut full-length released June 22 2015 on Dirty Hit Records, it was produced by Mike Crossey.

The band described the record as a “mix of genres” with singer Ellie Rowsell telling NME that “It’s 100 percent not a grunge record. It’s a much braver record than that”. Rowsell added: “We’ve been making sounds that don’t necessarily sound like guitar and experimenting with the way we use our voices. We’ve tried to match the emotion of the songs in how they’re performed and recorded, tried to make a sad song really feel and sound sad.”

The four-piece’s first work has sold over 60,000 copies, it was certified Silver by BPI and it gained a Mercury Prize nomination.

Visions Of A Life

The lead single “Yuk Foo” premiered on June 12th on BBC Radio 1, and was followed by “Don’t Delete The Kisses”, “Beautifully Unconventional”, and “Heavenward” before the release date of September 29, 2017. In a unique fashion, the song titles and the lyrics were teased through postcards sent to fans and the song order was progressively announced on Instagram, along with the pictures of the postcards.

Blue Weekend

During the interview they revealed that the title for the new album came from a cab journey in Belgium where they were recording the album. Vocalist/guitarist Ellie Rowsell suggested to drummer Joel Amey that the band should head to a nearby forest for a day out “on the next blue weekend”. “Blue is a nice colour, but it also means sad,” Rowsell told NME of the title. “And I often think the weekend is so fun, but lots of drama takes place then so sometimes it’s the catalyst for your downfall.”
